| XJTU-E04 | Snapshot rate length and cadence | At every one-minute interval, 32,768 samples per channel are recorded at 25.6 kHz, corresponding to a 1.28-second snapshot. | XJTU-S01 | p. 406, Section IV.A | High | Verified |
| XJTU-E05 | Direction-selection rationale | Because radial load is applied horizontally, the paper reports that the horizontal accelerometer captures more degradation information and uses that signal for RUL estimation. | XJTU-S01 | p. 406, Section IV.A | High | Verified |
| XJTU-E06 | Naturally developed endpoint damage under accelerated loading | The testbed produces degradation through operation rather than installing a seeded defect. | XJTU-E07 | Operational failure criterion | Tests stop for safety when vibration amplitude exceeds 20 g, and that time is treated as bearing failure time. | XJTU-S01 | p. 406, Section IV.A | High | Verified |
| XJTU-E08 | Speed interpretation | The dataset has three fixed operating speeds across experiments; the paper does not describe speed ramps within an individual run. `variable_speed` therefore means cross-condition variation, not continuously variable-speed acquisition. | XJTU-S01 | p. 406, Table I | High | Verified |
| XJTU-E09 | No reported bearing-rotor or bearing-gear compound condition | The documented experiment varies tested-bearing load and speed and reports bearing degradation; it does not introduce rotor or gear faults. | XJTU-S01 | pp. 405–406, Section IV.A and Figs. 2–3 | High | Verified |
| XJTU-E10 | Internal compound status unresolved | Several endpoint failure modes are mentioned, but the paper does not provide a bearing-by-bearing final-damage table proving that two internal elements fail simultaneously in the same sample. | XJTU-S01 | pp. 405–406, Section IV.A and Fig. 3 | High | Needs review |
