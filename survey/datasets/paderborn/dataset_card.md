# Dataset Card: Paderborn University

## Identity

- Dataset ID: paderborn
- Dataset name: Paderborn University
- Institution: Paderborn University
- Release year: 2016

## Purpose and scope

Benchmark dataset for bearing damage diagnosis in an electromechanical drive. Its principal research target is detection of an external test bearing using motor current, with synchronously collected bearing-housing vibration as a reference. It emphasizes systematic damage characterization and comparison between artificial and accelerated-life damage. [PU-E01, E03, E10]

## Acquisition setup

A modular drive rig contains a 425 W PMSM, torque-measurement shaft, test-bearing module, flywheel, and load motor. Thirty-two 6203 bearing instances—six healthy, twelve artificially damaged, and fourteen with accelerated-life damage—were measured under four fixed operating settings. This is a batch diagnostic dataset; the published records are not a run-to-failure time series. [PU-E02, E08, E09, E10]

### Measurement position and mounting rationale

One PCB Piezotronics 336C04 accelerometer measures the inner housing holding the test bearing through an adapter at the top of the rolling-bearing module, in the main radial-load direction. This gives a mechanical transmission-path rationale. The attachment method is not stated, and the paper does not compare alternative vibration positions. [PU-E04, E05, E15]

### Sampling rate, RPM, and record length rationale

The vibration and two motor-current channels are synchronized at 64 kHz, with 30 kHz and 25 kHz analog low-pass filters respectively. The authors explicitly cite high sampling rate above 40 kHz as a benchmark quality requirement. Each operating setting has twenty four-second measurements, giving 256,000 samples per high-rate channel per record. RPM conditions are 900 and 1500; torque and radial force are varied independently. These are verified PU conditions, not automatically justified UOS v2 settings. [PU-E06, E07, E08]

## Sensors and channels

- Vibration: one uniaxial piezoelectric accelerometer channel at one bearing-module location
- Motor current: two phase-current channels (`u`, `v` in Fig. 5)
- Supporting signals: speed, torque, radial force, oil temperature at unspecified lower rates
- Synchronization: main vibration/current signals and supporting operating signals are reported as synchronous; hardware-simultaneous ADC sampling is not established
- DAQ/A-D converter model: Unknown

Under this repository taxonomy, PU is multi-sensor, multi-modal, and synchronized, but its vibration data are not multi-position or multi-direction. [PU-E03, E04, E06]

## Operating conditions

Four one-factor-at-a-time settings are reported:

- 1500 RPM, 0.7 Nm, 1000 N
- 900 RPM, 0.7 Nm, 1000 N
- 1500 RPM, 0.1 Nm, 1000 N
- 1500 RPM, 0.7 Nm, 400 N

Temperature was maintained at approximately 45–50 °C. [PU-E08]

## Fault conditions and labels

Artificial bearings contain single-point IR or OR damage generated by EDM, drilling, or engraving. Accelerated-life bearings contain fatigue pitting and plastic-deformation/indentation damage with single, repetitive, or multiple distributions. Table 5 identifies KB23, KB24, and KB27 with both IR and OR damage; these meet this repository's same-sample internal bearing compound criterion. However, they are a small set of naturally developed multiple-damage bearing instances rather than a balanced designed matrix of IR+OR/IR+ball/OR+ball/triple combinations. Rolling-element damage was not observed, and cage, rotor, and gear fault conditions are not included. [PU-E10, E11, E12, E13]

## Data organization and access

The paper states that raw data are released as MAT files through the KAt-DataCenter under CC BY-NC 4.0. Filenames encode operating setting, bearing code, and repetition; uniform fact sheets describe bearing damage. The current official repository URL, exact deposited variables, lower-rate channel sampling, and file inventory still require verification. [PU-E16, E17, E18]

## Validation reported by source

Vibration envelope analysis verifies expected outer- and inner-race characteristic frequencies, shaft frequency, harmonics, and sidebands. The paper also reports machine-learning classification experiments using vibration and motor-current features; vibration classifications outperform motor-current classifications in the reported experiments. [PU-E14]

## Known limitations and conflicts

- Only one bearing model/type (6203 ball bearing) is used, although multiple manufacturers are represented. [PU-E09]
- Only one vibration position/direction is reported; mounting method and multi-position comparison are absent. [PU-E04, E05, E15]
- Internal IR+OR compound examples exist, but coverage is sparse and not a factorial compound-fault design. [PU-E11, E12]
- Supporting-channel sample rates, A/D converter model, exact MAT schema, code availability, and current repository version remain Unknown. [PU-E06, E18]
- Published diagnostic records are batch measurements of already prepared bearings, not degradation trajectories. [PU-E02]
