| PRO-E01 | Dedicated platform/challenge publication | The 2012 PHM paper introduces PRONOSTIA and the IEEE PHM 2012 bearing prognostic challenge. | PRO-S01 | paper pp. 1–2, Abstract and Section I; p. 7, Section V | High | Verified |
| PRO-E02 | Normally developed non-seeded degradation | Bearings begin without initiated defects and are accelerated by radial loading; the paper contrasts them with seeded-fault tests and calls them normally degraded. | PRO-S01 | paper pp. 3–4, Section II.B and Section III | High | Verified |
| PRO-E03 | Natural internal compound degradation | The paper states that each degraded bearing contains almost all defect types—balls, rings, and cage—and that degradation may concern all bearing components at the same time. | PRO-E04 | Two directions at one bearing position | Two miniature accelerometers at 90 degrees measure horizontal and vertical radial vibration on the tested-bearing outer race. This is multi-direction, not multi-position vibration. | PRO-S01 | paper pp. 4–5, Section III.C and Fig. 4 | High | Verified |
| PRO-E05 | Platform sensor rates | Acceleration is sampled at 25.6 kHz, temperature at 10 Hz, and instantaneous radial force, speed, and torque at 100 Hz. | PRO-S01 | paper pp. 4–5, Section III.C | High | Verified |
| PRO-E06 | DAQ description | Acquisition uses a four-slot NI cDAQ chassis containing three I/O modules and USB 2.0 transfer, but exact module/chassis models and timing topology are not given. | PRO-S01 | paper p. 5, Section III.C and Fig. 5 | High | Needs review |
| PRO-E07 | Three fixed-per-run operating conditions | Challenge conditions are 1,800 RPM/4,000 N, 1,650 RPM/4,200 N, and 1,500 RPM/5,000 N. | PRO-E08 | Six full and eleven truncated trajectories | Participants receive six complete run-to-failure learning sets and eleven truncated test sets across three conditions; physical lifetimes range from about one to seven hours. | PRO-S01 | paper p. 7, Table I and Section V.B | High | Verified |
| PRO-E09 | Fault type withheld | Challenge participants are given no assumption about the failure type of test bearings; the data do not provide a controlled diagnostic label matrix. | PRO-S01 | paper p. 7, Section V.B | High | Verified |
| PRO-E10 | Physics-model mismatch | The paper reports that classical bearing characteristic-frequency signatures and theoretical L10 life estimates do not match well, partly because multiple components can degrade together. | PRO-S01 | paper p. 6, Section IV.D; p. 7, Section V.B | High | Verified |
| PRO-E11 | Reported feature analyses | Example analyses include PSD, K-factor, crest factor, and wavelet packet decomposition, with both gradual and sudden/noisy degradation patterns. | PRO-S01 | paper pp. 5–7, Sections IV.B–IV.E and Figs. 8–14 | High | Verified |
